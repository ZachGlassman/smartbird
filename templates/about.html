Who created smartbird?

Photo credit : Rob Schroll, some flannel, and a nice brick wall

Hi, I'm Zach and smartbird is my capstone project as a Data Incubator Fellow. Lovingly crafted in the Bay Area, smartbird improves your flying experience.

I decided to create smartbird after flying accross the country regularly and noticing that that there were certain "things" I started to value more than just the price of the flight. How much do I care about being on time? Do I care how comfortable the seats are? Is my connecting airport good? I made smartbird to quantify the things you care abot and improve your flight experience.

Why do we need smartbird?

With smartbird, you can prioritize what is important to you and allow us to figure out how to translate that into a flight choice.

For example, when flying from JFK to SFO in Feb, Delta is great on average, but for those unfortunate times where Saturday travel is a must, you probably want to make another choice.

How does smartbird work?
  • React
    javascript framework by facebook used to build the user interface and interact with the backend API. Anything pretty on this site is probably because of React.
  • Webpack
    used for bundling, collecting and minifying. Sort of like my mother when I was a child, it makes sure everything is in the right place at the right time.
  • Yarn
    package management. This app relies on 695 javascript packages and Yarn makes that work.
  • Flask
    backend. Flask allows a python love like myself to craft everything with nice indentation and simple syntax and send it all to the the frontend when the users want to view it.
  • postgreSQL
    database. Every flight for the last year, airport information, etc. It all needs to go somewhere and get queried quickly, luckily, we have postgres.
Where does the data come from?
  • United States Bureau of Transportation Statistics
  • The Points Guy
  • Google Qpx
